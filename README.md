
# H1B Lottery Registration Automation

## 📌 Project Overview
During the H1B season, immigration attorneys face a high-pressure 14-day window to register hundreds of beneficiaries. The current manual process is error-prone, time-consuming, and carries a "zero margin for error" risk—where a single typo can cost a client their chance at the lottery for the entire year.

This project proposes a **hybrid automation solution** to streamline the workflow from data entry to USCIS submission.

## 🚀 The Problem
* **Time Intensity:** Attorneys spend 10-15 minutes per registration on "copy-pasting and praying."
* **High Anxiety:** USCIS error messages are vague and must be interpreted manually.
* **Scalability:** A single attorney handles 80-120 registrations across 20+ clients in two weeks.

## 🛠 Proposed Solution
The goal is to move from manual data entry to a system that automates the "grunt work" while keeping attorneys in control of final submissions.

### 1. What is Fully Automated?
* **Data Validation:** Automatic scanning of CSV uploads to catch formatting errors before they reach USCIS.
* **Field Mapping:** Direct mapping of client data to USCIS registration fields.
* **Error Interpretation:** Translating cryptic USCIS error codes into actionable instructions for the user.

### 2. What is Partially Automated?
* **Batch Uploads:** Grouping registrations by employer to minimize redundant logins and navigation.
* **Status Tracking:** Real-time dashboards to show which registrations are "Drafted," "Submitted," or "Error."

### 3. What Remains Manual? (The "Human-in-the-Loop")
* **Final "Submit" Click:** To maintain compliance and security, a human must perform the final review and click the submit button.
* **Payment Processing:** Attorneys manually handle the final fee payment to ensure financial records match.

## 📈 Success Metrics
* **Efficiency:** Reduce time per registration from 15 minutes to under 2 minutes.
* **Accuracy:** Achieve a 0% rejection rate due to data entry errors.
* **User Satisfaction:** Reduce reported "anxiety levels" from attorneys during the 14-day filing window.

---

### 💡 Why this matters
This automation isn't just about saving time; it's about **risk mitigation**. By automating the repetitive elements of the H1B lottery, we allow legal experts to focus on strategy and client advocacy rather than data entry.
